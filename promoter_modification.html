-10 and -35 Promoter Modification

Description

This is a continuation of Predictive Artificial Neural Networks for mRNA Transcriptional Profiles and Sequence Motifs. For more information, see the description and GitHub repository.

This research is ongoing and in the extension to my previous project, I will be working with Professor Matthew Tien in the Whitman College Biology department. There are five stages to extending this research, including computational and bench work.
Research Stages

In the first stage, I will develop the model to be more tailored to the cAMP receptor protein in E. coli. This will include data from Justin Kinney’s research, making the model more closely related to our summer research. I will develop ML approaches to predict sequence motifs closest to a desired transcription rate, make a more comprehensive comparison between CNN and RNN, and include transfer learning models.

In the second stage, I will design a GUI for the model based on functional requirements from Professor Tien and Professor Brit Moss. This will include a more accessible interface for predicting transcription rate and allow users to input data, improving the model’s accuracy.

In the third stage, I will conduct lab work with Professor Tien. This will include genetic engineering of E. coli σ70 promoter sequence motifs for further model validation and training. We will be measuring the expression of genes using the fluorescence spectra of colonies as a proxy. Fluorescence is not present in E. coli in situ, so we will be introducing foreign genetic material using gene delivery and artificial horizontal gene transfer.

In the fourth stage, Professor Tien and Professor Moss will use the model and GUI to teach the Cells, Molecules, & Mechanisms (BIOL-102) course. In the final stage, Professor Tien and I will write a research paper based on the model development results, genetic engineering lab work, and results from using the model as a teaching tool.
